What AsyncGuard Catches

90 checks across 6 categories, covering every aspect of async/await correctness and promise safety.

PM
💥

Promise/Future Misuse

Detects unhandled rejections, the promise constructor anti-pattern, floating promises, .then() without .catch(), dangling promise chains, and async callbacks that silently swallow errors.

15 checks
AR
💧

Async Resource Leaks

Finds unclosed connections in async functions, missing finally blocks, event listeners without cleanup, streams opened but never closed, and database handles that leak on rejection paths.

15 checks
EL
🛑

Event Loop Blocking

Catches synchronous I/O in async contexts, JSON.parse on large payloads without streaming, CPU-bound work blocking the loop, heavy computation in request handlers, and recursive sync calls.

15 checks
CA
🚫

Cancellation & Abortion

Detects missing AbortController usage, fetch calls without timeout signals, uncancellable async operations, orphaned background tasks, and request handlers that cannot be interrupted gracefully.

15 checks
AE
🔥

Async Error Patterns

Catches swallowed errors in catch blocks, error objects losing stack traces during re-throws, unhandled rejections in background tasks, empty catch clauses, and async try/catch anti-patterns.

15 checks
AC
🔀

Async Coordination

Finds race conditions in state updates, missing Promise.allSettled() where partial failures matter, sequential awaits that could run in parallel, and unsafe shared mutable state across async boundaries.

15 checks
